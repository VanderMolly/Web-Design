The Basics

What's in a tea party:

Why tea parties? Why not! It's something different and it's fun to make stuff, decorate and get dressed up for a party. It's a certain kind of coordinated you don't normally get in a normal party and it's something everyone can contribute to and enjoy.

History:

What are the origins of tea parties? The first tea party, on record, was in England in 1662. It was for the celebration of a royal marriage. Throughout the 1700s tea was enjoyed by English nobility, at the end pf the century coffeehouses began to open in England and tea and coffee became more accessible.

Tea parties increased in popularity from the 1800s-1900s. As tea parties transitioned from the nobility to the common people they because less elaborate and more informal. This created the difference between afternoon tea and high tea

Interestingly enough high tea was actual for the common class, whilst afternoon was for the nobility.

Eventually tea parties gained traction in other parts of the world. In the United States the tea traditions stem from Britians influence from colonial times.

Types of Tea Parties

Theres not just one kind of tea party, traditionally and technically there are different kinds. You are also always welcome to throw a themed one or one for a specific occasion. But if we're talking textbook tea party, here are the kinds.

Afternoon:

A more casual tea, consists of tea, finger sanwhiches and hors d'oeuvres. (small dishes/finger foods), scones and petite desserts.

High Tea:

Heart dinner, of meat, bread and vegetable and tea. High tea is tea served with a meal/

Low Tea: Tea served with a snack, something in between meals.

There are other kinds of parties, but for a tea party you would probably host one of these three kinds of tea parties.
